# BELIZE CORE KNOWLEDGE - Training Corpus

## GOVERNMENT & LEGAL

Belize is a parliamentary constitutional monarchy and Commonwealth nation. The official language is English. The capital is Belmopan, while Belize City is the largest city. The country gained independence from the United Kingdom on September 21, 1981.

The Financial Services Commission (FSC) regulates financial institutions in Belize. All banks, credit unions, and money service businesses must comply with FSC guidelines and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations.

BelizeID is the national digital identity system that integrates Social Security Numbers (SSN) and passport information. Citizens can use BelizeID for KYC verification when opening bank accounts or accessing government services.

The Land Registry maintains official records of all property ownership in Belize. Land titles are recorded by district: Belize, Cayo, Corozal, Orange Walk, Stann Creek, and Toledo.

## ECONOMY & CURRENCY

The Belize Dollar (BZD) is pegged to the US Dollar at a 2:1 ratio. One US dollar equals two Belize dollars.

BelizeChain introduces the DALLA token as the native cryptocurrency for the national blockchain. DALLA rewards validators through Proof of Useful Work (PoUW) consensus.

The bBZD is a stablecoin pegged 1:1 to the Belize Dollar (BZD), governed by the central bank through the Economy pallet. The peg maintains monetary stability for domestic transactions and international trade.

BelizeX is the national decentralized exchange (DEX) for trading digital assets including DALLA, bBZD, and tokenized real estate.

Tourism is Belize's largest industry, contributing over 40% of GDP. The country is famous for the Belize Barrier Reef, the second-largest coral reef system in the world, and the Great Blue Hole.

## LANGUAGES & CULTURE

Belize is one of the most culturally diverse nations in Central America. The main ethnic groups are:
- Mestizo (Spanish-Maya mix): 52% of population
- Creole (Kriol): 25% of population
- Maya: 11% of population (Q'eqchi', Mopan, Yucatec)
- Garifuna: 6% of population
- Mennonite: 4% of population
- East Indian, Chinese, and others: 2%

Languages spoken in Belize:
- English (official): 62% of population
- Spanish: 56% of population
- Kriol (Belizean Creole): 44% of population
- Maya languages (Q'eqchi', Mopan): 9% of population
- Garifuna: 3% of population
- German (Mennonite communities): 3% of population

Kriol phrases:
- "Weh di gat?" = What's up? / What do you have?
- "Fi true?" = Really? / Is that true?
- "Ah di yaad" = At home
- "Unu" = You all
- "Da how lang?" = How much? / How long?

September Celebrations commemorate Belize's independence. The festivities include the Battle of St. George's Caye Day on September 10th and Independence Day on September 21st.

## GEOGRAPHY

Belize is located on the eastern coast of Central America, bordered by Mexico to the north, Guatemala to the west and south, and the Caribbean Sea to the east.

The six districts of Belize are:
1. Belize District - Includes Belize City (former capital)
2. Cayo District - Includes Belmopan (capital), San Ignacio
3. Corozal District - Northern border with Mexico
4. Orange Walk District - Sugar cane region
5. Stann Creek District - Includes Dangriga, Hopkins
6. Toledo District - Southernmost district, Maya heartland

Major landmarks:
- Great Blue Hole: UNESCO World Heritage Site, famous diving location
- Belize Barrier Reef: Second largest in the world
- Cockscomb Basin Wildlife Sanctuary: World's first jaguar preserve
- Caracol: Ancient Maya archaeological site
- Actun Tunichil Muknal (ATM Cave): Sacred Maya cave

## LEGAL FRAMEWORK

Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements in Belize mandate that all financial institutions verify customer identity using BelizeID or government-issued identification. Business accounts require additional documentation including business registration certificates.

Anti-Money Laundering (AML) regulations require reporting of suspicious transactions over $10,000 BZD to the Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU). Banks must maintain transaction records for at least seven years.

Data sovereignty laws require that all government and citizen data be stored on infrastructure located within Belize or on Belizean-controlled blockchain systems.

## TECHNOLOGY TERMS

Substrate: Blockchain framework developed by Parity Technologies, used as the foundation for BelizeChain.

WebAssembly (WASM): High-performance runtime used for BelizeChain's on-chain logic.

Polkadot: Multi-chain network that BelizeChain can connect to via XCM (Cross-Consensus Messaging).

IPFS: InterPlanetary File System, decentralized storage protocol used by Pakit.

Arweave: Permanent data storage network integrated with Pakit for archival documents.

Proof of Useful Work (PoUW): BelizeChain's consensus mechanism that rewards validators for federated learning contributions based on Quality (40%), Timeliness (30%), and Honesty (30%).

## EDUCATION

The University of Belize (UB) is the national university with campuses in Belmopan and Belize City. The university offers programs in agriculture, business, education, engineering, health sciences, and liberal arts.

The Belize Education System uses British-based standards with primary school (ages 5-14), secondary school (ages 14-18), and tertiary education. The Caribbean Examinations Council (CXC) administers standardized exams.

## BUSINESS & COMMERCE

Starting a business in Belize requires:
1. Register with the Companies Registry
2. Obtain Trade License from local municipality
3. Register for General Sales Tax (GST) if applicable
4. Open business bank account with KYC documentation
5. Register with Social Security Board for employee contributions

The General Sales Tax (GST) in Belize is 12.5% on most goods and services.

Import duties vary by product category, ranging from 0% to 65%. CARICOM member states receive preferential rates.

## NATURAL RESOURCES

Belize's main exports include:
- Sugar (largest agricultural export)
- Bananas
- Citrus fruits (oranges, grapefruit)
- Marine products (lobster, conch, fish)
- Crude petroleum
- Papayas

The Mennonite community contributes significantly to agriculture, particularly poultry, dairy, and grain production.

Sustainable forestry produces mahogany, cedar, and rosewood for export while protecting rainforest ecosystems.

## HEALTHCARE

The Belize Healthcare System includes:
- Karl Heusner Memorial Hospital (KHMH) in Belize City - national referral hospital
- Western Regional Hospital in Belmopan
- Northern Regional Hospital in Orange Walk
- Southern Regional Hospital in Dangriga
- District hospitals and rural health posts throughout the country

The National Health Insurance (NHI) program provides affordable healthcare coverage to Belizean citizens.
